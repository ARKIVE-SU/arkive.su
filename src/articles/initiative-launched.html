Initiative launched — outreach begins to 200+ institutions worldwide

The Knowledge Ark Initiative has officially begun its global outreach campaign. Over 200 institutions across 6 continents have been contacted — including national libraries, internet archives, digital preservation platforms, university repositories, and cultural heritage organisations.

The initiative operates as a decentralised coordination network, connecting three types of participants who rarely interact: knowledge holders (libraries and archives), storage technology developers (ceramic and crystal media), and infrastructure providers (space missions and bunker operators).

At this stage, the campaign is focused on interest-mapping — identifying institutions willing to participate in a dialogue about long-term knowledge preservation. No financial commitments are requested.

Every previous civilisation believed its knowledge was safe — until libraries burned and media degraded. We are the first generation with the technical means to break this cycle forever.

Updates will be posted as responses are received and partnerships form.
