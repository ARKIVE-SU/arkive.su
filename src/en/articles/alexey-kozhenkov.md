In the effort to preserve cultural heritage, documenting living architecture plays a vital role. We are honored to announce that **Alexey Kozhenkov**, a prominent architectural photographer, has reached out to us. He is a master whose work transforms concrete and glass into frozen symphonies of geometry and light.

For many years, Alexey has specialized in photographing Modernist and Brutalist architecture. His artistic vision and technical excellence have earned him recognition across Europe and beyond. His works are frequently featured in world-leading publications like [ArchDaily](https://www.archdaily.com/photographer/alexey-kozhenkov) and exhibited in international galleries.

## A Contribution to Future Heritage

Alexey Kozhenkov has decided to provide his extensive archive, spanning 15 years of international shoots, to the **Knowledge Ark** project for eternal preservation. This collection includes thousands of unique frames capturing the disappearing aesthetics of cities worldwide—from the utopian housing blocks of Hong Kong to the stark Brutalist forms of Belgrade and Berlin.

> "Those who preserve cultural heritage must necessarily familiarize themselves with those who are actively creating that heritage for the future," note the Knowledge Ark curators.

Documenting architecture is more than just taking beautiful pictures. It is the fixation of the state of human space at a specific point in time. Many of the structures Alexey photographs are prone to decay, redevelopment, or oblivion. Preserving his archive on ultra-reliable media ensures that centuries from now, our descendants will be able to see the architectural world through our eyes.

## About Alexey's Work

Alexey Kozhenkov's creativity has been recognized with prestigious awards, including an Honorable Mention at the **MonoVisions Photography Awards**. His acclaimed series, such as *"Spaces for Winds"* and *"Ambient Metropolis Light"*, explore the relationship between the monumentality of buildings and the fluidity of the urban environment.
