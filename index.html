Three Pillars of Preservation

Knowledge Holders

National libraries, internet archives, museums, digital repositories — the institutions that curate and safeguard humanity's intellectual heritage.

Storage Technology

Ceramic media, 5D crystals, and other ultra-durable formats designed to outlast civilisations — no power required, immune to radiation and electromagnetic pulses.

Infrastructure

Lunar data centres, deep mountain bunkers, salt mine vaults — geographically and physically distributed storage locations on Earth and beyond.

How It Works

Connect

We map and connect knowledge holders, technology developers, and infrastructure providers worldwide.

Curate

Institutional experts select the most important records — science, culture, art, language, history.

Encode

Selected knowledge is written onto ultra-durable media: ceramic, crystal, and digital formats.

Distribute

Copies are placed in maximally dispersed, protected locations — underground vaults, mountain bunkers, the Moon.

The Formula

Content  +  Permanent Storage Media  +  Preservation Infrastructure

Multi-redundancy across media types, geographic locations, and formats — analogue and digital, Earth and space.
